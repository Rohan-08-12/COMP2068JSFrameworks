What you need to know about spa!

A spa is a location where mineral-rich spring water (and sometimes seawater) is used for medicinal baths. Spa towns or spa resorts typically offer various health treatments (balneotherapy). The belief in the curative powers of mineral waters goes back to prehistoric times. Such practices have been popular worldwide, especially in Europe and Japan. Day spas are also popular and offer various personal care treatments.

  1. Origin of the term
    • Bathing in Greek and Roman times
    • Bathing in Medieval times
    • Bathing in the 18th century
    • Bathing in the 19th and 20th centuries

Origins of the term

The term is derived from the town of Spa, Belgium, known since Roman times as Aquae Spadanae. Some connect it with the Latin spargere (“to sprinkle or moisten”). Others suggest it comes from the Belgian town where a curative natural spring was discovered in the 14th century.

Treatments

A body or spa treatment is a non-medical procedure to support the health of the body. It is often performed at a resort, destination spa, day spa, beauty salon, or school.

Recent trends

In the modern world, spa therapies are linked to beauty, pampering, indulgence, and health. The spa industry continues to grow and increasingly embraces wellness as its core.

Types of treatments

  • Day spa — a form of beauty salon
  • Destination spa — a resort for personal care treatments
  • Spa town — visited for the supposed healing properties of the water
  • Foot spa
